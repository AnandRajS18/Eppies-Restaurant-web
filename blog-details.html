Chef's Notes • May 14

Sourcing the Perfect Local Truffles

By Executive Chef Michael

Join our head chef on a journey through the coastal highlands as we partner with local foragers to bring you the freshest black truffles for our signature wagyu sliders.

The coastal highlands offer a unique microclimate that is absolutely perfect for the growth of winter black truffles. For generations, local families have walked these hillsides with their trained dogs, feeling the earth and respecting the delicate balance of the forest ecosystem. At Eppies, we believe that bringing this tradition to your plate is a privilege.

The Art of Foraging

Early mornings in the mist, the smell of damp earth, and the sudden, exciting alert from a truffle dog – these are the moments that define true culinary exploration. We don't just order ingredients from massive distributors; we build relationships with the earth and those who understand every nuance of its shifting seasons.

"A great dish doesn't start in the kitchen. It starts in the soil, the sea, and the careful hands of those who respect nature's timing."
— Chef Michael

Bringing It Home to Eppies

Once sourced, the truffles are rushed back to our kitchen, where they are gently cleaned and preserved for the evening service. Shaved freshly over our signature dishes like the acclaimed wagyu sliders, the earthy aroma fills the dining room before the plate even hits the table, creating an experience that is intensely local yet timeless.

Come join us this weekend to taste the harvest. Supplies are always limited, as we only serve what the season generously provides. Make a reservation today to guarantee your share of this seasonal delicacy.
